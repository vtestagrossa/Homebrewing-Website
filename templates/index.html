Why homebrew?

Homebrewing beer is a hobby I picked up while locked away at home during the pandemic. I think it's a fun hobby that can be relatively inexpensive to start, and takes little more than knowledge of cooking to get started. Having said that, you can wind up spending a lot of money on equipment, especially if you're trying to establish control over the environment of the yeast and consistency in your brewing process.

  • You learn a marketable skill.

  • You can have the kind of beer you like every time.

  • You'll probably make way more beer than you need. A typical batch is about 5 gallons, or 53 beers, so you can share it with friends.

  • The startup cost for brewing is relatively inexpensive for a hobby.

  • It's fun!

There are definitely other ways to spend your money, and the recurring costs can add up over time (such as buying grains, replacing bottles, getting a kegerator and CO2 setup), especially if you decide to expand the hobby, but I think it's worth doing if you like craft beer. We're in a time where what used to require a special trip to a homebrew shop now can be done online.
